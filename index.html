About

I am a PhD student in Information Systems at The University of Queensland (UQ), where I am fortunate to be advised by Dr Morteza Namvar, Assoc Prof Saeed Akhlaghpour, Prof Marten Risius, Assoc Prof Andrew Staib, and Prof Andrew Burton-Jones.

Prior to joining UQ, I obtained a MCom dgree in Data Analytics for Business from The University of Sydney and a BSc dgree in Economics.

My current research interest focuses on designing AI artifacts, particularly large language models (LLMs)-based, to address significant methodological gaps in healthcare. Specifically, I collaborate with Metro South Health and its leading hospitals in Australia to develop AI-driven tele-triage models. The models are designed to guide patients toward the emergency facilities that best meet their clinical needs. I am honored to have my PhD research project partially funded by PA Research Foundation. Besides, building on my early research experience, I am also interested in online safety topics with a sociotechnical nature on social media.
